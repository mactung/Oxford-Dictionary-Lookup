# Privacy Policy for Oxford Dictionary Lookup

**Last Updated: March 17, 2026**

Oxford Dictionary Lookup ("we," "our," or "the Extension") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use our Chrome Extension.

## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1 Personal Data
When you create an account for Cloud Sync, we collect:
- **Email Address**: Used for account identification, password recovery, and unique user identification.
- **Username**: Used to display your name on the Global Leaderboard.

### 1.2 Learning Data
To provide the core features of the Extension, we store:
- **Vocabulary List**: Words you have looked up and saved.
- **Learning Progress**: SRS levels, review history, and quiz scores.
- **Settings**: Your preferences for quiz intervals and UI themes.

### 1.3 Browse Data
The Extension **DOES NOT** track your browsing history. We only process the text you explicitly select to look up a definition.

## 2. How We Use the Information
- **Cloud Sync**: To synchronize your vocabulary and progress across multiple devices.
- **Leaderboard**: To display your ranking based on your learning effort.
- **Service Improvement**: To debug issues and improve the parsing of dictionary data.

## 3. Data Storage and Security
- **Local Storage**: Data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's `storage` API.
- **Cloud Storage**: Synced data is stored on our secure server (`oxford.ipamaster.site`) using industry-standard encryption.
- **Passwords**: We do not store plain-text passwords. All passwords are hashed using bcrypt before being saved to our database.

## 4. Third-Party Services
We fetch dictionary data from the Oxford Learner's Dictionary website. Their privacy policy applies to those interactions. We do not share your personal email or learning data with any third parties.

## 5. Your Rights
You can view, modify, or delete your account and data at any time through the Extension settings.

## 6. Disclosure for Chrome Web Store
The Extension collects and uses "Personal Communications" (Email) and "Personal Identification" (Username) only to provide the cloud sync and leaderboard features as described above.
